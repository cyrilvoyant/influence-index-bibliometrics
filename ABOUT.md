# Influence Index

The Influence Index is a scale-independent metric designed to quantify institutional research concentration using the Top 2% scientists framework.

Unlike traditional bibliometric indicators based on publication volume or citation counts, it measures the proportional representation of highly influential researchers and expresses deviations from a neutral baseline on a logarithmic scale, enabling direct and symmetric interpretation.

Developed by Cyril Voyant, Jeroen Baas, Roy Boverhof, Paolo Stringari, Mathilde Marchand-Lasserre, and John P. A. Ioannidis, the index is intended for institutional self-evaluation, strategic analysis, and longitudinal assessment without reliance on ranking-based comparisons.
